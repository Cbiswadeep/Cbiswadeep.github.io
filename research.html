Research on Spiking Neural Networks

Temporal Processing

Content related to how spiking neural networks' (SNNs) unique temporal processing capabilities are leveraged for various applications.

Parallel Computing

Insights into the integration of the parallel computational attributes of spiking networks into existing architectures to enhance efficiency and performance.

Unsupervised Graph Learning

Exploration of the development and application of spike-based unsupervised learning techniques in graphical neural network models.

My research focuses on harnessing the temporal processing capabilities of Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) for the advancement of Artificial Intelligence applications. This research posits that the appropriate utilization of the temporal processing power of SNNs can facilitate the development of next-generation AI technologies, characterized by enhanced performance, robustness, and cost-efficiency.

Research Focus Areas

Utilizing the Temporal Information

Processing of SNNs to pioneer innovative applications, while simultaneously exploiting the heterogeneity in time scales inherent in SNNs to attain functionalities and applications that are unattainable with traditional neural networks.

Combining the Parallel Computational Nature

Of spiking networks with innovations in Neural Architecture Search with temporal feedback connections. The research will utilize SNN-specific NAS optimization for energy-efficient, distributed learning at the edge, particularly focusing on tasks like distributed optimization, event-based sensing, etc.

Applications of SNNs

Like solving combinatorial optimization problems on large-scale dense dynamic graphs and event-based vision models for low-power edge devices.
